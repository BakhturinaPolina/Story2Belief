# 03 — Three-year dissertation plan

The PhD is framed as **phased expansion** of the 3-month prototype, not a
single giant build.

## Year 1 — Build and validate

- Curate a small literary corpus across genres and moral explicitness.
- Stabilize the narrative-analysis pipeline; benchmark BookNLP, SentiArt,
  and moral-extraction prompts against manual annotations.
- Freeze v1 of the `ReaderProfile` schema and validate against published
  individual-difference findings (transportation, empathy, NFA, NFC).
- Outputs: methods paper on the narrative pipeline; preregistered
  simulation protocol.

## Year 2 — Systematic simulation studies

- Vary text features (POV, realism, conflict, moral explicitness) and
  reader traits (empathy, transportability, prior beliefs) factorially.
- Estimate text × reader interaction effects.
- Move agent stack to Concordia for memory + reflection.
- Outputs: 2 papers (text-feature effects; reader-trait effects); first
  full empirical chapter.

## Year 3 — Longitudinal effects, transfer, and writing

- Model **sleeper effect**: source-memory decay vs. retained semantic
  content.
- Compare literary vs. non-literary controls.
- Compare narrative vs. counter-narrative exposure (resistance to
  persuasion).
- Outputs: dissertation chapters, capstone paper on long-term moral
  transfer.

## Cross-cutting commitments

- **Open methodology**: each pipeline release accompanied by versioned
  prompts, schemas, and a reproducibility script.
- **Negative controls**: at least one factual non-narrative text per
  experimental block.
- **Theory grounding**: every new variable is anchored in narrative
  persuasion or moral-foundations literature before being implemented.

## Open questions

- _Where does human-subject validation fit?_ Likely a small
  comparison study late Year 2 / early Year 3, not a primary deliverable.
- _Multilingual extension?_ Russian-language stories are a natural fit
  given the indoctrination angle; decide in Year 1.
