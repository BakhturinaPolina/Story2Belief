# ADR 0004 — Prototype scope: 2 texts × ≤50 readers × 1 transfer task

- **Status**: Accepted (2026-05-16)
- **Context**: The prototype must demonstrate the architecture
  end-to-end within 3 months. A larger scope risks failing the timeline;
  a smaller scope fails to demonstrate the architecture.
- **Decision**: Bound the prototype as follows:
  - **2 texts**: one fairy tale, one realistic short story, chosen to
    maximize contrast on realism, story-world distance, and moral
    explicitness.
  - **20–50 reader-agents** with theory-constrained synthetic profiles.
  - **1 transfer task** per agent (pre/post pair), drawn from existing
    moral-narrative corpora.
- **Consequences**:
  - All five committee-facing outputs (schema diagram, feature table,
    cohort table, transfer-delta plot, methods memo) are reachable in
    12 weeks.
  - Statistical claims at the prototype stage are descriptive, not
    confirmatory. That is appropriate for a feasibility demonstration.
  - Year 1 of the dissertation expands corpus, cohort size, and
    transfer-task battery.
- **Alternatives considered**: 1 text × 100 readers (loses
  text-feature contrast); 5 texts × 20 readers (multiplies costs and
  failure surface area).
